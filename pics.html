PICS UI Design

Overview

PAMS is a customer facing web application that enables users to manage the deinstallation, storage, and resale of their redundant telecommunication network hardware. I was brought on as a UI Design intern to help the IT team make PAMS more appealing to PICS customers in both look and feel.

The Problem

The old PAMS was unintuitive and resource intensive. Customers weren't using it because they found it was easier to just contact PICS directly if they needed something done. This cost PICS hours of personal customer service man-hours.

The Process

I started sketching basic pages with Google Material Design in mind. Material Design was an ideal choice for a design system for PAMS. Since I was only going to be available for the summer, the IT team needed a design system they could learn on their own and implement with pre-built components. The sketches helped me settle on basic layout ideas.

LoFi prototypes were created to convey layout and navigation to the team. The team and I also noticed repeatedly that we didn't have enough requirements from the customers as to what information is necessary to display. I used LoFi designs to meet requirements as they came in because iteration was quick and easy.

Working with Material Design made quick iterations easy, so continued work was done solely with HiFi prototypes.

The Result

By the end of the internship, requirements were still messy, but development had to begin. I assisted with implementing Material Design in Angular. I took on the role of helping frontend development, so I was able to dabble in more web development as well as design.

This was my first experience in making real designs for a real company. I learned how valuable it is to make sure requirements are as concrete as possible before starting designing. If I had better requirements at the beginning, I would have not needed to iterate as many times. My biggest takeaway from this experience was that I truly do like UI design. This internship killed any imposter syndrome I had.

As of now I am still working part time at PICS Telecom to help the team complete the project and stick to the design I envisioned.
